Autonomous Vehicles

When people are talking about autonomous vehicles these days, the first thing that comes to mind for a lot of people is a self-driving Tesla vehicle on a freeway somewhere in California or in some cases on the side of that freeway burning down with nothing to stop the toxic fire. The biggest issue for self-driving vehicles is the perception of safety of those vehicles in comparison to other vehicles in traffic which are still operated/governed by a human driver. (Plungis, 2019)
In order to understand the topic “autonomous vehicles” and the technology behind we have to examine the 6 different official levels of automated driving according to the international standard J3016 by the SAE and the “Levels or Driving Automation”.
Level 0 - 2 consider the driver as the main operator of the vehicle while Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS) support and engage on different levels at different points in time depending on the feature. Level 0 can also be referred to as “no automation”, however the SAE considers even at this level features like auto emergency breaking or blind spot warning systems to be present. (SAE International, 2019)
Level 2 features may control steering and speed of the vehicle via different systems, however, the human driver must remain engaged in all driving tasks and monitor traffic. The most common examples can be found in several vehicles today and include but are not limited to self-parking assistant systems as well as lane keeping assist or traffic jam assist. The latter is a combination of lane centring and adaptive cruise control. (Lim, 2019)
The SAE differentiates level 3-5 from 0-2 by the fact that in vehicles that offer these functionalities “You are not driving when these automated driving features are engaged - even if you are seated in “the driver’s seat”. (SAE International, 2019)
A very prominent example which caused one of the main discussions about safety and autonomous vehicles is the Tesla AutoPilot which is nominally classed as level 3 autonomy. Several incidents have been reported including fatal collisions. (Schmelzer, 2019)
Level 5 is considered fully autonomous and does not even require a steering wheel and accelerator or break pedal. The main distinguishing factor to level 4 is that the vehicle is able to perform the driving task in any condition at any speed. Google’s Waymo project is at the forefront and already offers a small fleet of driverless taxis in California which are considered level 4. (Alford, 2019)
The underlying hardware components to enable driverless vehicles and allow for automated driving are cameras, Light Detection and Ranging (LiDAR) systems, localisation sensors or radars and digital maps which in combination create the input dataset for the the on-board computer in any autonomous vehicle. (Coppola, 2019)
An example of such a dataset for the Waymo project is currently available online with Google also publishing tutorials on GitHub on how to use the data in order to help programmers to build research models. Such datasets, which have also been released by other companies like Lyft, are publicly available and licensed for non-commercial use only. (Alford, 2019)
While autonomous vehicles are seen to be governed by the vehicle itself and are therefore not per se connected to their environment, a different category called CAV (Connected Automated Vehicles) might help autonomous vehicles to achieve mainstream status and close important safety gaps. CAVs add V2X networking and connectivity solutions to the ADAS features that are already existing in those vehicles. V2X (Vehicle to everything) describes different networking solutions and refers to high-bandwidth, low latency and highly reliable communication of vehicle to vehicle (V2V), vehicle to infrastructure (V2I), vehicle to device (V2D) and vehicle to pedestrian (V2P) communication. Those solutions will rise in significance in the next 5-10 years with the rollout of 5G networks around the world. (Mueck, 2019)
Lastly, the technology for autonomous vehicles is mostly used in everyday commute situations. However, other applicable scenarios for the application of autonomous vehicle technology are also found in the racing industry. NIO, a Chinese electric vehicle manufacturer, has showcased what the technology can do in a road legal electric supercar. The following link shows a world record for the fastest self-driving car at the Circuit of the Americas in Austin, Texas.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dAQ_7fQu8IQ

The transition to autonomous vehicles will have a significant impact on our transport ecosystem. While there are different theories about the time frame of this transition reaching from 10 to 50 years, there is the consensus that this technology will prevail and make our roads safer and our daily commute more efficient. And the entry into this market can be approached quite differently. While Tesla is introducing gradually more autonomous features in Tesla vehicles, Waymo is already equipping vehicles with Level 5 autonomy but controlling the circumstances (e.g. weather, day or night etc.). Conventional vehicle manufacturers are trying to enter the market place in the form of cooperations and partnerships with software companies. As a result, BMW has partnered with Intel and Mobileye, General Motors with Lyft and FCA with Waymo. The biggest hurdle at this stage seems to be regulations in each individual market. While level 5 autonomy technology might be widely available by 2025, regulatory issues could delay a mass market entry. (Coppola, 2019)
Autonomous vehicles will affect a very large group of our society and pose not only a technological change, but an economic, social and cultural change. A successful introduction will allow certain groups in society access to mobility which they have never had before. Children, old people, disabled or sick would have the opportunity to take part in a mobile life again. However, despite the euphoria for autonomous vehicle technology, there are many objections at the same time. People are concerned about the appeal of Motorsport with automated vehicles and question the superiority of autonomous vehicles over an actual race car driver on the track. (Herrmann, 2018)
More importantly, people question the safety of autonomous vehicles, in particular in the transition period when driver governed vehicles and driverless cars share our roads together. The expectations of people towards driverless vehicles are a complete disappearance of accidents. However, Tesla already mentioned that the Autopilot will not be able to accomplish that, but it makes crashes less likely to occur and therefore makes the world a safer place for vehicle occupants, pedestrians and cyclists. (Plungis, 2019)
The industry is still shaped by a tremendous growth in jobs with US job postings alone climbed 833% from 2015 to 2019. The top 3 companies are Aptiv, Nvidia and SAIC Innovation Centre and represent mostly technology companies. Most wanted skills in those companies are programming skills in C or C++, Python and Linux but also software development, Matlab, machine learning and ADAS. (Mendoza, 2019)

From a personal perspective this will have quite a significant impact on my life but even more so on my childrens’ lives. In the near future nearly every manufacturer will embark on the autonomous vehicle journey and will try to establish a positioning in this fairly new market. As an employee of an automotive manufacturer I will experience the effects of autonomous vehicles on the industry first hand. While I’m exposed to the technology as such in my professional life it will be the social and cultural change in my personal life that might even be more affected. With autonomous vehicles around the corner the impact on everyday commuters will be quite significant. Not only will it allow a safer travel to any destination it will also free up time for work, leisure or other things while on the commute. It will also bring down the cost of transportation quite significantly up to a point where it might become free of charge. This will impact my everyday travel from A to B but will also reduce shipping cost for purchases or other delivered items. Furthermore, it will introduce the cabin of a vehicle as a completely new living space and the extension of my lounge room for example. Infotainment availability and connectivity services will flourish next to in-vehicle gaming and AR/VR applications that use the motion of the vehicle as an enhancer for user experience.
In a latest forecast the autonomous vehicle industry is expected to grow to a total of $556.67 billion in 2026 and will certainly push the transition from ownership to mobility-as-a-service. (Allied Market Research, 2019)
This means there are a lot of opportunities in new business areas and with new skill sets required, IT degrees will attract more students than ever before.
In the end autonomous vehicles will have a lasting impact on society by revolutionising the way we travel, making transport services more customisable, from door to door and on demand at all times. (Coppola, 2019).

IT Technologies

Ras Pi ect.

Humanity has entered the age of the fourth industrial revolution. Technology once only available to large corporations are now affordable for the backyard inventers and many open source projects exist enabling technology enthusiast to create a wide variety of custom systems with small computers at their heart. These computers are growing cheaper and more powerful every day and have a large community of hardware and software hackers spearheading technological innovation. The scope of this report will be exploring the world of consumer maker devices and what they can do for us now and in the future.

Arduino Since the release of the Arduino in 2007 there has been an explosion of popularity and interest in cheap single board computers. Arduino is a family of single board computers with many input output capabilities in fact there is an array of prebuilt add on technologies for these boards. Although a fantastic device it stops short of being a computer in the sense of what the average person considers a computer. Limited to 16mhz the processor although still being highly useful is not powerful enough to run sophisticated operating systems. It however is extremely cheap and versatile for robotics and embedded systems. The use cases for this technology are vast from simply turning on a heater when the temperature falls below a set limit to building a voice activated smart home. A vast array of pre-built add on devices called hats are available that expand. The only real drawback of these devices is programming knowledge is a must or at the very least the willingness to learn. More information can be found at: https://www.arduino.cc/

Raspberry Pi When Raspberry Pi hit the small computing scene in 2012 it landed with a bang. I fully functional single board computer with fully programmable inputs and outputs. It evolved fast and grew an almost cult following of technology enthusiasts. Raspberry PI has become a very powerful single board computer capable of running a wide variety of sophisticated operating systems. Raspbian is one of the most popular choices of operating system, it is a slightly modified version of Linux distro Debian. As with Arduino there are many add on hats and accessories coupled with the input capabilities and multiple programming languages the most popular being python. This is a dream come true for anyone wanting to build fully functional robots with easy to use graphical user interfaces. For the non-programmer this is still a great little computer with lots to offer as the latest pi 4 has the option of 4gb of ram allowing for 4k video and the running of larger programs. More information can be found at: https://www.raspberrypi.org/

Makey Makey

Mackey Makey is an educational electronics toy launched on Kickstarter in 2010 with the final prototype complete in 2012. This device is an add on to the personal computer allowing for customisable interactions. Allowing children to create a vast array of fun electronics out banana’s, cardboard, family pets in fact almost anything and everything can be used to explore this world. This would be the starting point for introducing children to electronics and a lot of laughs for adults as they learn alongside their children. Children are our future and the more we can get interested in these fields the brighter our future will be. If a young Steve Woznick didn’t have hands on experience building circuits the would not have apple computers and devices.
More information can be found at: https://makeymakey.com/
World Impact Cheap accessible tools for the general population means more people will be able to develop their ideas on tight budgets. New technologies will emerge as a direct result of this. Right now, all over the world back yard engineers are working on robotics projects and even collaborating through online platforms such as git hub to advance this field. Its an exciting time to be a maker and the fourth industrial revolution will bring about a glorious age for mankind There will likely be a few drawbacks as mass adoption of these cheap systems will likely impact businesses that have been creating computers for the consumer markets for the last few decades will have to share the market that they once dominated with contenders like Raspberry Pi although competition is great for innovation and consumers of this technology.

Personal Impact For myself the availability of this technology is rather profound I have wanted to build robots my entire life and this technology has enabled me to do this without needing to build circuit boards. Within 10m of where I sit writing this report I have 2 Raspberry Pi’s one that is connected to an Arduino based grbl board that runs a CNC milling machine that I use in my workshop the other is going to serve the exact same function on another machine that will be fully operational in about a week. As owner operator of a business like this having robotic hands to do the repetitive tasks is of great benefit. I also have many Arduinos which I have built many projects including mobile robots and telemetry sensors. I am currently designing a custom-built robot that will eliminate all the manual labour from one of my business functions which will likely be Arduino based. What this technology enables me to do is operate multiple machines and produce my products without the need for manual labour. For my customers it delivers value for myself it generates profit. I hope to build many of these systems over the course of my lifetime and eventually have factories full of robots producing fantastic products at great prices as well as employ people to help me in these endeavours. I understand that this is rather ambitious but without great ambition nothing great is ever achieved. My family and friends are already getting benefits from these systems as they are the first customers for a lot of my new products and are making requests for custom products regularly. Since most of the labour is done by machines the products that would take days to make by skilled wood workers are done precisely in hours.
